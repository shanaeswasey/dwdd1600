Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship is the idea of using technology and the internet responsibly, safely, ethically, and effectively.

What is Digital Law?

Digital law refers to the rules, rights, and legal responsibilities connected to using technology, the internet, and digital content.

What is AUP?

AUP stands for Acceptable Use Policy. It's a set of rules that explains how people are allowed — and not allowed — to use a computer system, network, website, internet service, or device.

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is presenting someone else's words, ideas, work, or creations as if they were your own without giving proper credit.

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty is cheating or being unfair in schoolwork, such as plagiarism, copying answers, or using unauthorized help.

What are the rules for AI Generated Content?

The rules for AI-generated content are to use AI responsibly, honestly, and legally. People should not present AI-created work as entirely their own if it is not allowed, and they should follow school, workplace, or platform guidelines. AI content should also be checked for accuracy, avoid plagiarism, respect copyright and privacy laws, and never be used to cheat or spread false information.
